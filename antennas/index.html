EFHW deployment guide for EN11xe
Context: From central Nebraska, you want to reach the eastern and western US simultaneously for POTA hunters. The key variable is which direction the apex points and whether you want NVIS (close contacts <500mi) or skip (500–2000mi). Sunset greyline runs roughly NE–SW through EN11xe — orienting your sloper along that axis maximizes contacts during the window.
Inverted-V (apex up, ends down)
40m45–55° NVIS · best for 200–600mi hunters

Best for: parks where you need to work everyone, don't know the propagation direction. Apex at 25–30′ is the sweet spot — below 20′ and 40m goes almost pure NVIS.
NE–SW Sloper (apex NE, feed end SW)
40m30–40° toward NE · reaches Northeast + Gulf
30m22–32° · strong toward New England
20m15–22° · good DX lobe NE direction
17m12–18° · DX-capable toward EU via NE path
Best for: morning activations targeting eastern hunters. Greyline alignment. Apex at tree, feed end at ground level or low support.
E–W Sloper (apex E, feed end W)
40m30–40° toward E · reaches East Coast well
30m22–30° · good East Coast
20m15–20° · good East + West in upper lobes
Good compromise when you want both coasts. Less NVIS than inv-V, more directional than you might expect — EN11xe is centered enough that E–W covers a lot of population.
Flat-top (both ends level)
40m55–70° pure NVIS · <300mi radius
30m40–55° regional NVIS
20m30–40° · marginal skip on 20m
Only use this if your park has two trees at the same height and you're targeting local/regional contacts. Poor for skip on 40m from EN11xe.
Low sloper (apex 15′, end 3′)
40m50–65° · mostly NVIS even as sloper
30m40–50° · regional
20m25–35° · still decent skip
When you only have a single low support (picnic table post, short tree). 20m works reasonably well. 40m will be regional/NVIS. Better than nothing by a large margin.

Run ~20′ vertical from feedpoint then fold horizontal. Low-angle 40m champ. Needs at least 2 short radials (17′ each) or a ground screen.
Counterpoise and coax analysis — 17′ vertical / Rybakov
Ground system comparison
Ground system40m loss vs ideal20m lossNotes
CHA FSR ~1 dB <0.5 dB <0.05Ω Cu-Ni fabric. 23–28% better field strength than 4×12.5′ wire radials per Chameleon field data. Integrated 3/8-24 mount for Rybakov/MPAS.
Door screen (aluminum, ~36×80″) ~1–1.5 dB <0.5 dB Larger continuous area than FSR strips. Comparable RF performance. ~$15. Corrodes at fold lines over time. Awkward to pack.
4× quarter-wave wire radials (17′ each) ~2–3 dB ~1–2 dB Better than nothing, worse than screen or FSR. Tripping hazard. 8+ radials closes the gap.
No ground plane 3–6 dB 2–4 dB Coax braid becomes the counterpoise — erratic, depends heavily on coax length. Common-mode current on feedline. Not recommended on 40m.
Coax length without counterpoise — 17′ vertical
Coax lengthEffective as CP?40m behaviorRecommendation
25′ (7.6m) Marginal ~0.18λ at 7 MHz — too short and too long for predictable resonance. High CM current, variable loss. Don't rely on it
15′ (4.6m) Poor ~0.11λ — very reactive, unpredictable. SWR swing, tuner may struggle. Add real ground plane
10′ (3m) Poor Essentially no counterpoise. Antenna won't load efficiently on 40m. Always use ground plane
33′ (10m) — ~λ/4 at 7 MHz Acceptable Quarter-wave coax resonates as CP on 40m — intentional coax counterpoise technique. Still noisy vs screen. Usable emergency fallback
Bottom line: Random coax lengths without a deliberate ground plane give unpredictable results on 40m. Your door screen or CHA FSR solves this completely. The 33′ coax trick is a known POTA emergency technique but not a substitute for a real ground plane.
Head-to-head: door screen vs CHA FSR
SituationBest choiceReason
Drive-up park, operating from tableDoor screenNo pack penalty, more area, costs nothing extra
Hike-in activationCHA FSRTwo small bundles vs awkward 36″ tube — no contest
Public park with foot trafficCHA FSRVisible strips, soft edges, survives being stepped on
Using Rybakov / MPAS systemCHA FSRIntegrated 3/8-24 adapter — direct mount
Wet / muddy groundCHA FSROxford outer layer resists moisture; screen corrodes at fold lines
Budget-constrainedDoor screen~$15 vs ~$90
The RF difference between the two is small — probably indistinguishable at QRP levels on any given contact. Carrying both for different activation types is a rational strategy: door screen in the car bag, FSR in the hiking pack.
